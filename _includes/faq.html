FAQ

  • What is MinneHack?
    MinneHack is a hackathon that welcomes hackers from around the country — a 24-hour event where you can compete, create, and collaborate to make something amazing. If you make something outstanding, you can win prizes and recognition from our sponsors! Even if you don't win, we will be giving out swag from our sponsors during the event!
  • Who hosts MinneHack?
    MinneHack is hosted by the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities chapter of the Association for Computing Machinery. If you have any questions about the event, you can contact us at acm@umn.edu, or use the contact form below.
  • When is MinneHack?
    MinneHack 2017 will start on Saturday, January 28th at 6PM CST and run for 24 hours until Sunday, January 29th 6PM CST.
  • Where is MinneHack?
    The Great Hall in
    Coffman Memorial Union, University of Minnesota, 300 Washington Ave SE, Minneapolis, MN 55455.
  • How do teams work?
    You can create your own team ahead of time if you'd like, or meet and greet new people and form a team at the event! Teams will consist of 1-4 undergraduate, graduate, or high school students of any skill level and any discipline, but all are judged on the same grounds, so the more the merrier!
  • Can I work on something before the event and bring it to MinneHack?
    What you present at MinneHack must all be created within the 24 hours of the event. However, feel free to brainstorm and bring your ideas!
  • How do the prizes work?
    Prizes are doled out on a per-person basis. A winning team of 1 member gets 1 prize. A winning team of 4 members gets 4 prizes! So there's nothing to lose for having a full team.
  • How much does MinneHack cost?
    Thanks to our wonderful sponsors, MinneHack is completely free! We will provide food, drinks, internet, entertainment, swag from our sponsors, and even a sleeping area, so all you have to worry about is the coding.
  • What do I need to bring?
    You'll need to bring yourself and a computer! A more comprehensive list of things will be sent to you before the event, but we also recommend:
    • Phone
    • Charging cables
    • Hygiene products
    • Blankets
    • Medication
  • Why do you want my résumé?
    First and foremost, MinneHack is a recruiting event. Our sponsors have paid top dollar to see you, the hackers! So submit your resume, stop by the sponsorship booths, and talk to our mentors and judges! Even if you're not in the market for a job right now, you will be someday; what better way to start a conversation than at MinneHack?
  • What's the internet situation like?
    This year we are able to provide both wired and wireless internet! We recommend you bring a device compatible with ethernet, as the wi-fi at our venue cannot account for the load of hackers we attract. We will provide ethernet cables. If you need a converter, we have a few available on first-come first-serve basis, but we recommend you bring your own.
  • Will you be providing transportation?
    As we get closer to the event, we will let you know whether a bus will be running through your school or not. If we aren't sending a bus your way, we will reimburse you for your travel expenses, up to $100. Just be sure to hold onto your receipts!
  • What if I don't have enough experience to compete. Can I still attend?
    The most important thing is that you're there, have fun, and learn something. Go ahead and register and make the most out of it. It's completely free, so there's nothing to lose!
